Oven roasted sweet potatoes

The sweetest gift from the ground

Sweet potatoes are roasted to perfection with chunky onion wedges and garlic, then finished with a drizzle of balsamic vinegar for a naturally sweet holiday side dish. My Granny made this for Easter dinner. We were all sitting at the table commenting on how delicious it was and asked for the recipe.

Ingredients

  • Sweet Potatoes:

    This recipe calls for four cups of peeled, chopped sweet potatoes. Depending on the size of your spuds, you'll need four to six sweet potatoes to get four cups.

  • Onions

    You'll need one sweet onion (such as Vidalia) cut into wedges to roast along with your sweet potatoes.

  • Garlic

    Two cloves of thinly sliced fresh garlic give these roasted sweet potatoes an extra burst of flavor.

  • Olive Oil

    Toss the sweet potatoes, onions, and garlic in high-quality olive oil before roasting to prevent sticking and to keep the veggies moist.

  • Balsemic Vinegar

    Balsamic vinegar is drizzled over the vegetables after roasting, adding irresistible depth of flavor and subtle acidity.

  • Salt and Pepper

    Finish these oven-roasted sweet potatoes with salt and ground black pepper.

The Steps:

  1. Gather all ingredients
  2. Preheat the oven to 425 degrees F (220 degrees C).
  3. Mix sweet potatoes, onion, and garlic in a bowl. Drizzle olive oil over the mixture and toss to coat; pour into a shallow roasting pan.
  4. Roast sweet potato mixture in the preheated oven, turning frequently, until the vegetables are soft and golden brown, 30 to 35 minutes.
  5. Remove from the oven. Drizzle balsamic vinegar over the vegetables; season with salt and pepper.
